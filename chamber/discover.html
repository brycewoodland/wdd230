Downtown Salt Lake City

Salt Lake City was founded on July 24, 1847 when the pioneers from the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints traveled to Utah. There is a reason why the streets in Salt Lake City are so wide. Brigham Young, leader of the Church of Jesus Christ, asked that they be built as such to allow u-turns for the oxcarts.

Antelope Island

Antelope Island is the largest of ten islands surrounding the Great Salt Lake. Antelope Island is a scenic beauty and holds the populations to several animals. This includes bighorn sheep, American bison, porcupine, badger, coyote, bobcat, mule deer, and waterfowl.

Hiking

Salt Lake City is home to many beautiful hikes.
Easy (family-friendly): Cecret Lake, Donut Falls, Ensign Peak, Silver Lake, City Creek, and Jordan River Parkway.
Moderate: Great Western, Bonneville Shoreline, Lake Blanche, and Dog Lake.
Strenuous: Alexander Basin, Mount Raymond, Desolation Lake, Mount Van Cott, and Mineral Fork.

Skiing or Snowboarding

It's also the location for great skiing or snowboarding! Check out locations such as Brighton, Snowbird, Park City, Alta, etc. A great experience when the weather gets colder!

Downtown Salt Lake City

Salt Lake City's beautiful skyline! Things to do in Salt Lake City include Temple Square, Natural History Museum of Utah, Utah State Capitol, This is the Place Heritage Park, City Creek Center, Clark Planetarium, Hogle Zoo, and Loveland Living Planet Aquarium.

Salt Lake City Mountains

Among all the wonderful things to see and do in Salt Lake City, don't forget to enjoy the beautiful and spectacular mountains.
